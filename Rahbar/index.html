Faqs

Frequently Asked Questions.

Who can go to Umrah?

  1. Any Muslim who is either a US citizen, or permanent resident (Green card holder), or with a H1/ L1 visa.
  2. Any male who’s age is >=18.
  3. Any male who’s age is < 18 but is accompanied by a mehram.
  4. Any female who’s age is >= 45 and has a notarized No objection letter attached from a mehram.
  5. Any female who’s age is < 45 and is accompanied by a mehram

What Advice Do You Have For Women Who Want To Do Umrah?

All women are required to travel for Umrah with a Mehram (male accompanying a female whose age must be at least 16). Proof of kinship must be submitted with the application form. Women over age of forty-five (45) may travel without a Mehram with an organized group. They must, however submit a no objection letter from their husband, son, or brother authorizing them to travel for Hajj with the named group. That letter should be notarized.

Umrah Visa Requirements?

  • 2 White Background Passport Size Photographs (with names written on the back).
  • Passport validity must be at least 6 months.
  • If not a US passport holder, then proof of residence is required i.e. either the green card copy, or H1/L1 visa copy and the passport should have a stamp of entry.
  • Meningitis Vaccination.
  • Marriage Certificate Copy (If Applicable).
  • Birth Certificate Copy for Kids (If traveling with kids).
  • Visa Application completely filed out for each traveler.

Information Needed For Umrah?

  1. Name as per passport (First/Middle/Last )
  2. Date of travel
  3. State of residence
  4. Number of adults
  5. Number of children (under 12 years)
  6. Duration of stay
    1. Makkah
    2. Madinah
  7. Package required (economy/deluxe)
  8. Transport (One way: Airport to Hotel/Return: Airport to Hotel and Hotel to Airport/Return plus Mazarat: Return transport to hotel along with transport to Mazarat)
